\chapter*{Abstract}

Shared micromobility services have experienced a significant rise in popularity, leading to high usage of these services.
This increase in popularity came with challenges regarding the service quality.
Due to the distinct usage patterns of such services, fleet imbalances occur, which decrease the quality of services and necessitate a dynamic rebalancing strategy.
This thesis proposes a novel Hierarchical Reinforcement Learning (HRL) framework to address these challenges.
The framework decomposes the problem into two tiers: a high-level Regional Distribution Coordinator (RDC) for operator-based rebalancing and a set of low-level User-Incentive Coordinators (UICs) for user-based incentives.
The high-level RDC focuses on strategic movements that address imbalances over the whole service area, while the UIC agents address local imbalances.
The framework was evaluated in a custom simulation environment against multiple baselines.
