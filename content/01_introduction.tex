\section{Motivation}
Shared micromobility services, such as electric scooter sharing, are a large, growing part of urban and suburban mobility.
Shared micromobility offers an ecologically friendly and convenient way to bridge short distances.
They address the common problem of the "first and last mile gap", which describes the distance between public transport stations and the trip's origin and destination.
The growing popularity of micromobility sharing services comes from their flexibility, ease of use, low environmental impacts, and the possibility of reducing private car rides.
To fulfill all these benefits from a user perspective, the operators of such sharing services face challenges in keeping service quality high.
Rebalancing is a common approach to maintaining a balanced fleet of vehicles and, therefore, providing a high service quality.
Fleet rebalancing refers to the operation of moving vehicles from low-demand areas to high-demand areas to increase satisfied user demand.
Traditional rebalancing strategies often only focus on periodically rebalancing operations based on static demand models.
These rebalancing strategies miss out on the dynamics of shared micromobility services, where user demand fluctuates and is highly dynamic.
Demand can peak unexpectedly due to public events, which static rebalancing strategies can not account for. Therefore, there is a need for rebalancing strategies that can react to such unexpected demand fluctuations.
A simulation environment that can reflect the highly dynamic environment of such sharing services is a key enabler to test and evaluate such rebalancing strategies.


\section{Research Gap and Objectives}
The literature often uses highly abstract simulation environments to evaluate rebalancing strategies. 
These can not simulate the dynamics of sharing services that are needed to develop rebalancing strategies that can react to dynamic changes in user demand.
Therefore, this thesis focuses on developing a fine-grained and dynamic simulation environment to train and test rebalancing strategies that can operate in real-time and offer suggestions on optimizing the fleet.
Additionally, this thesis develops multiple rebalancing strategies using reinforcement learning, heuristics, or other techniques for testing in the developed simulation environment.
